About Eden's Rose Foundation

Mission Vision

To relieve the suffering of the most impoverished and marginalized people in the world. Our work to end the cycle of poverty through community-wide empowerment and sustainability projects is rooted in the belief that all people need and deserve the basic provisions of access to economic opportunity, nutritious food, financial education, and a safe sanctuary.

Micro Projects

Produce Pickup Project

Free produce hand out, operating Monday through Thursday every week. Run by neighborhood residents in the West Hill of Albany, NY. The goal is to address the food desert of historically poor and underserved people.

Wheels Up

A program for teaching kids how to fix their own bikes and get access to free new ones. This gives them skills to use in life, gets them active, and keeps them away from drugs and guns in the West Hill of Albany.

Albany Victory Gardens

Free plots of land for urban farmers to grow their own food to increase nutrition access, increase investment into the community, and turn previously unused and unsafe spaces into arable land that serves the community in the West Hill.

Urban Farmers Training Program

A three-year program that's free for everyone to learn how to be an urban farmer. Graduates receive their own micro farm.

Micro Farms

Creating small farms in Albany for people to grow their own food to serve themselves and the community, and increase land ownership in an area of 70% historic renters.

Youth Blueberry Orchard

A blueberry orchard in the West Hill that's grown by and tended by youth to increase nutritional access to produce, helping develop healthy lives for kids and have their hands in something real while still young.

Frontlines

Using the first 5 feet of every plot we have to grow native, endemic, and pollinator-friendly plants to beautify the West Hill, increase access to passive green space, and support the pollinator habitat.

SEVA

In Ecuador, teaching native people how to grow their own cacao and create great chocolate according to the historic sacred practices of their culture.

Albany Apiary

Beekeeping to support native plants and agriculture. Albany youth are involved in harvesting.

Macrame

Ecuadorian women keeping the ancient art of macrame alive and offering them financial freedom through a job to keep them from selling their own rainforest land to make ends meet.
