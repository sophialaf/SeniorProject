Welcome to the Albany Victory Gardens!

Albany Victory Gardens was established to fight for food equality
through the development of spaces that enable teaching,
recreational to subsistence gardening, and access to food
for urgent community food needs in the West Hill neighborhood of Albany.

Beginning during World War II, victory gardens were established to combat
food shortages, boost moral and self respect, engender a sense of
independence among the unemployed, and to promote patriotism. Again in the
1970â€™s, a resurgence grew due to soaring food prices,
awakening consciousness to environmental issues, and neighborhood
revitalization movements. Victory gardens emphasized social
connectedness and grassroots activism.

While the Albany Victory Gardens has grown over
the last seven years into a streamlined food systems project,
the foundation is still rooted in providing food security and
economic independence. The fundamental goals for the garden still exist
at the forefront of our efforts.

Experienced gardeners and new participants share garden
plots to develop a more sustainable community by working
together and sharing in the fruits of their collective labors.
The tradition of sharing knowledge and experiential gardening wisdom
can bridge social, economic and diversity gaps as well as enable self
reliance and access to nutritious food. AVG was designed to recuperate
the culturally pertinent, economic viability of gardening through capacity
building (teaching/learning) and strengthening the tradition of sharing
gardening experience.
